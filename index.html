Gagarin Yuri Alekseevich

Soviet Air Forces pilot and cosmonaut who became the first human to journey into outer space.

Here's a time line of Yuri Gagarin's life:

  • 1934 - Yuri Gagarin was in the village of Klushino, near Gzhatsk.
  • 1941 - Klushino was occupied during the German advance on Moscow and a German officer took over the Gagarin residence
  • 1943 - His two older siblings were deported by the Germans to Poland for slave labour.
  • 1946 - The family moved to Gzhatsk, where Gagarin continued his secondary education.
  • 1950 - Gagarin began an apprenticeship as a foundryman at a steel plant in Lyubertsy, near Moscow, and enrolled at a local "young workers" school for seventh-grade evening classes.
  • 1951 - From both the seventh grade and the vocational school with honours in mouldmaking and foundry work, he was selected for further training at the Saratov Industrial Technical School, where he studied tractors.While in Saratov, Gagarin volunteered at a local flying club for weekend training as a Soviet air cadet, where he trained to fly a biplane, and later a Yak-18.
  • 1955 - Gagarin was accepted to the First Chkalovsky Higher Air Force Pilots School in Orenburg.
  • 1957 - Gagarin began flying solo.
  • 1957 - Gagarin was commissioned a lieutenant in the Soviet Air Forces having accumulated 166 hours and 47 minutes of flight time.
  • 1959 - He was rated Military Pilot 3rd Class.
  • 1960 - Gagarin was further selected for an accelerated training group, known as the Vanguard Six or Sochi Six,from which the first cosmonauts of the Vostok programme would be chosen.
  • 1961 - On 12 April , at 6:07 am UTC, the Vostok 3KA-3 (Vostok 1) spacecraft was launched from Baikonur Cosmodrome. Aboard was Gagarin, the first human to travel into space.
  • 1968 - While on a routine training flight from Chkalovsky Air Base, Gagarin and flight instructor Vladimir Seryogin died when their MiG-15UTI crashed near the town of Kirzhach.
